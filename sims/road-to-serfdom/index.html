The Road to Serfdom

An interactive reading of Hayek's argument (after the 1945 comic): how central planning trades freedom for control. Scroll the story, then take the dial. Canvas 2D · ideas.

1 · A free people

Millions of individuals, each pursuing their own ends — a riot of different goals, coordinated by no one in particular.

2 · The single plan

To plan the whole economy, one collective goal must replace the many. Individual ends get bent toward the center.

3 · We can't agree

No majority agrees on the one plan in detail. The planners stall — and a cry rises for a strong leader who can "get things done."

4 · The strong man

Power concentrates around the plan. Dissent is no longer mere disagreement but an obstacle — to be removed.

5 · Your dial

Coordination and freedom trade off. Move the dial and watch the cost. How far would you go?
